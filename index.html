THE SPORT

Soccer, also known as football in most countries, is the world's most popular sport. Played by two teams of 11 players, the objective is to score by getting the ball into the opponent's goal using any part of the body except the hands and arms. The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end, and it's governed by the rules of the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA).

HISTORY

Ancient Origins

Forms of soccer-like games date back to ancient civilizations. One of the earliest documented versions comes from China around the 2nd and 3rd centuries BC, known as "Cuju" (meaning "kick the ball"). Cuju was played with a leather ball filled with feathers, and the aim was to kick the ball into a net.

Modern Soccer Takes Shape

Modern soccer began to take its current form in England in the mid-19th century. Various schools and universities had their own versions of the game, but in 1863, the formation of the Football Association (FA) in London formalized the sport. This is considered the official birth of soccer as we know it today. The FA established standard rules, distinguishing soccer from rugby, which allowed players to use their hands. British sailors, merchants, and workers played a crucial role in spreading the game to Europe, South America, and beyond. By the late 19th century, soccer clubs had formed across Europe and South America, most notably in Italy, Spain, Argentina, and Brazil.

HOW ITS PLAYED

  1. Teams: Each team has 11 players, including 1 goalkeeper and 10 players (defenders, midfielders, forwards).
  2. Field: The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end.
  3. Game Duration: A match is typically 90 minutes long, divided into two halves of 45 minutes each, with a halftime in between.
  4. Scoring: A goal is scored when the ball crosses completely over the goal line, between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar.
  5. Winning: The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins. If the score is tied, it can either end as a draw or go into extra time or a penalty shootout, depending on the competition.

MAJOR COMPETITIONS

  • FIFA World Cup: Held every four years for both men and women.
  • UEFA Champions League: The top European club competition.
  • English Premier League (EPL), La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga: Top domestic leagues in Europe.

RULES

Rules Description
Offside Rule A player is offside if they are closer to the opponent's goal than the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is played to them.
Yellow Card A caution given to a player for unsporting behavior or persistent fouls.
Red Card Results in a player being sent off the field for serious foul play or violent conduct.
